Project Overview

Used car tires represent one of the most significant and rapidly growing sources of rubber waste in urban and industrial areas. These tires pose a serious environmental challenge due to their resistance to natural decomposition and the presence of harmful chemical compounds.

In line with CALX's commitment to developing sustainable environmental solutions, this project offers an innovative approach focused on recycling used car tires and converting them into valuable materials for various applications‚Äîsuch as rubberized asphalt, playground flooring, construction materials, and ground insulation.

The project utilizes advanced technologies for tire dismantling and processing, while ensuring minimal environmental impact and promoting economic value. It also supports national sustainability goals and aligns with Saudi Vision 2030.

The Global Challenge

  • An ongoing environmental issue affecting more than 25 cities worldwide
  • Rubber waste causes harm to human health and the environment (water, soil, air, and ecosystems)
  • Over 20 million car tires produced in Saudi Arabia since 2021
  • Around 10 million tires replaced annually since 2012
  • Less than 20% of this volume is recycled each year

Real World Events

  • Tire fire incident at Al-Ahsa landfill, Saudi Arabia (2021)
  • Massive tire fire at Kuwait landfill (2012)
  • Traditional tire burning contributes to up to 20% of pollution levels
  • Yanbu, Saudi Arabia (2019): Tire landfill fire caused severe environmental damage
  • Nigeria: Open burning of tires leads to major respiratory health issues

Project Solution

Project Ideation

The idea for this project emerged from the urgent need to address the growing problem of used car tire waste in urban and industrial areas. These tires pose significant environmental and health risks due to their non-biodegradable nature, chemical composition, and the dangers associated with improper disposal‚Äîsuch as uncontrolled fires and pollution of soil, air, and water.

Inspired by successful global and regional examples‚Äîand particularly by alarming incidents such as the Al-Ahsa and Kuwait tire fires‚Äîthis project was conceptualized to introduce an innovative, scalable, and eco-friendly recycling unit developed by CALX. The aim is to turn a persistent environmental hazard into a valuable resource that supports sustainability goals, aligns with Vision 2030, and contributes to the circular economy.

The project's technical solution is centered around the development of a specialized pyrolysis-based processing unit for used car tires. This advanced technology allows for the thermal decomposition of tire components in an oxygen-free environment, enabling the recovery of reusable by-products such as industrial-grade oil, black carbon, and steel.

Fixed Unit

Installed at strategic locations near landfill sites to reduce transportation costs and improve operational efficiency. Ideal for industrial zones with high tire waste generation.

Mobile Unit

Mounted on a dedicated truck to serve remote or rural areas, providing on-site recycling capabilities where centralized systems are not accessible.
